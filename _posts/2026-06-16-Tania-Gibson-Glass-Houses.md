People who live in glass houses should not throw stones, yet Mayor Tania Gibson has been busy hurling them at Taylorville Resource Park (TRP) with remarkable vigour. For some time, she has been a vocal protagonist warning Grey District residents about the alleged threat TRP poses to our local water supply. Yet, while pointing fingers downstream, she has completely overlooked that her own Grey District Council is now facing legal action over allegedly failing to supply safe drinking water.

The irony deepens as TRP has officially filed a Statement of Claim in the High Court in Christchurch, suing Mayor Gibson for defamation. The lawsuit centres on statements made by the Mayor implying that the park’s operations were polluting the Grey River and directly affecting the quality of the Council’s drinking water intake. In their claim, TRP argues that Gibson’s public comments—specifically regarding turbidity and pollution levels in the river—were false and damaging to their reputation as a business.

Gibson’s public statements have consistently painted Taylorville Resource Park as a looming environmental hazard. She has repeatedly claimed that the park’s operations jeopardise water quality, using these warnings to bolster her re-election campaign and rally local support. The narrative was simple: TRP is risky, the Council is vigilant, and Greymouth needs strong leadership to protect its taps. Unfortunately for this storyline, much of the evidence supporting these claims has been thin on the ground, with independent assessments suggesting the park’s impact on water quality remains well within acceptable limits.

Meanwhile, a rather different story is unfolding in court regarding the Council itself. Taumata Arowai, New Zealand’s Water Services Authority, has taken the Grey District Council to task for allegedly failing to supply safe drinking water to its communities. The regulator’s action centres on systemic issues with water treatment and monitoring—issues that directly affect public health. In other words, while Gibson accuses TRP of endangering our water through external pollution, her own council is being held accountable by the government for not properly safeguarding it in the first place.

It seems Mayor Gibson wants to hunt with the hounds and run with the fox. On one side, she courts votes by vilifying a local business; on the other, she presides over an administration that struggles to meet basic water quality standards. The irony is not lost on Greymouth residents who simply want clean, reliable drinking water. Until the Mayor turns her scrutiny inward rather than outward—defending herself against TRP’s lawsuit while answering to Taumata Arowai — her campaign against Taylorville Resource Park will remain a masterclass in political deflection. After all, you cannot blame others for the puddle on the floor when your own roof is letting the rain through.
